# General Writing Guidelines — PSM Report

## Before Submitting Your Draft

- Proofread your draft before submission and correct minor errors such as **typos**, **spelling errors**, and **formatting mistakes**. Drafts containing these errors will be returned for correction, requiring you to reprint.
- If writing in English, you are responsible for ensuring the report is **grammatically error-free** — either by proofreading yourself or seeking a proofreader.

---

## General Principles

- The purpose of a thesis/report is to **inform the reader** about what you have done throughout your final year project process. Write as **clearly, concisely, densely, and readably** as possible.
- Report **what you did**, not what is already in textbooks or common knowledge.
- Avoid long, sprawling sentences.
- Use **formal sentences and statements** that carry a single, unambiguous meaning.
- Every new or specialised term must be **fully defined** in a glossary/list of terms.
- Ensure **continuity** between sentences, between paragraphs, between sections, and throughout the report as a whole.
- Prioritise content by importance — important material comes first and is given emphasis and focus.
- **Primary/main content** belongs in the body of the report; supporting material goes in appendices.
- Every figure and table **must have a title**. Every figure and table also requires an explanation. Reference figures/tables by name in the text (e.g. *Figure 1.1 shows …*). **Not**: *The figure below/above shows …*

---

## Common Grammar & Formatting Mistakes

### Spacing
- There must be **exactly one space** between every word, and after every full stop or comma before starting a new sentence.
- **No space** before a full stop or comma.
- Extra spaces between words make the text look untidy.

### Word choice
- Avoid first-person pronouns ("I", "we") in the report. Use more formal constructions instead.
- Avoid using colloquial or informal filler words/particles in formal academic writing.
- Avoid sentences that are ambiguous or carry more than one meaning.

### Proofreading symbols used by supervisors

| Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `||` (two lines) | Add a space |
| `|` (one line) | Remove a space |
| **? and underlined word/phrase** | Review the suitability of the word/phrase in context |
| `?` (standalone) | The reviewer does not understand this |
